What's Inside

10+ fully written, customizable templates covering the complete freelance engagement lifecycle:

  • Master Service Agreement (MSA) — Your foundational contract. Covers the ongoing relationship between you and a client: general terms, intellectual property, confidentiality, liability limitations, dispute resolution, and termination. Use this as your base and attach project-specific scopes as addenda.
  • Project Contract (Fixed-Price) — A complete project agreement for one-time engagements with a defined deliverable and a fixed fee. Includes scope, timeline, milestones, payment schedule, revision limits, and acceptance criteria.
  • Project Contract (Hourly/Retainer) — For ongoing or time-based engagements. Covers hourly rates, retainer minimums, billing cycles, overage handling, and monthly reporting expectations.
  • Scope of Work Template (Detailed) — A standalone SOW document you can attach to any contract. Defines deliverables, specifications, timeline, milestones, assumptions, exclusions, and acceptance criteria. This is the document that prevents "but I thought it included..."
  • Scope of Work Template (Simple) — A streamlined one-page SOW for smaller projects. Project name, deliverables, timeline, price, payment terms. Sign and start.
  • Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA) — Mutual NDA template covering confidential information, duration, exclusions, and remedies. Written for freelancer-client relationships, not corporate M&A.
  • Project Brief Template — A discovery document you fill in with the client before writing the SOW. Captures goals, audience, brand guidelines, technical requirements, timeline expectations, and budget range. Prevents misalignment before the project starts.
  • Change Order Form — The document that saves you from scope creep. When a client requests work outside the original scope, you fill in this form: description of the change, impact on timeline, additional cost, and client approval signature. No more free work disguised as "small tweaks."
  • Payment Terms Clause Library — 8 pre-written payment clause variations you can drop into any contract: net-15, net-30, milestone-based, 50/50 split, deposit + final, retainer billing, late payment penalties, and kill fee language.
  • Kill Fee Clause — A standalone clause with three variations (percentage-based, milestone-based, and flat fee) that protects you when a client cancels mid-project. Includes guidance on when to use each variation.
  • Project Completion & Handoff Document — Formalizes the end of an engagement. Confirms all deliverables were received, final payment is due, and the project is officially closed. Prevents clients from coming back months later expecting free revisions.
