Headline

Run your job search like a project, not a prayer. Track every application, prep every interview, manage every relationship, and evaluate every offer — all in one organized Notion workspace.

Sales Description

Job searching is a full-time project. Most people manage it like it isn't.

They apply through LinkedIn and forget to follow up. They go into interviews without knowing what questions they'll ask. They have a great networking conversation and never log the person's contact info. They receive an offer and have to evaluate it without a clear sense of their own priorities.

Then they wonder why the process feels out of control.

The Job Search Command Center is the system that puts you back in the driver's seat. It's a complete Notion workspace purpose-built for managing a serious job search — one where you're applying strategically, preparing thoroughly, following up consistently, and making decisions from a position of clarity rather than desperation.

Whether you're actively applying for the first time in years or navigating a layoff with a hard timeline, this template gives you the structure to stay sharp, organized, and in control throughout the whole process.

What's Inside

5 interconnected databases that cover the full search lifecycle:

  • Applications Tracker — Every role you apply to, with full status tracking from Researching through Offer. Built-in formulas flag follow-up dates, calculate salary midpoints, and compare offers against your target. The Kanban view shows your entire pipeline at a glance.
  • Company Research — Deep-dive notes on every company you're considering. Glassdoor ratings, culture notes, tech stack, recent news, pros/cons, and an Overall Score so you're applying strategically, not scattershot.
  • Interview Prep — Create a prep record for every interview stage. Link it to your application, note the interviewers, plan your STAR stories, build your question list, and log your post-interview reflection. Includes a full STAR Story Bank with a library of your best behavioral examples.
  • Networking Tracker — Every professional contact that matters to your search. Log interactions, set follow-up dates (with auto-alerts for overdue outreach), track introductions requested and submitted, and identify who's connected to your target companies.
  • Resume Versions — Track every version of your resume, what it's optimized for, and which applications used it. Never lose track of what you sent where.

Offer Evaluation Matrix — A structured comparison worksheet for weighing compensation, benefits, role quality, growth potential, and personal priorities side by side. Pre-built to use when you have one offer or several.

Salary Comparison Table — Track every offer, salary estimate, and market data point in a single view, alongside compensation research resources organized by industry.

Weekly Goals & Reflection Template — A fill-in-the-blank planning sheet to run your search with weekly intention. Set application targets, log follow-ups due, and reflect on what's working — because job searching without feedback loops is how months disappear.

Dashboard with at-a-glance views: active pipeline, upcoming interviews, follow-ups due, networking outreach needed, applications by status chart, and salary comparison — all in one place.

Quick-start guide that walks you through setup step by step, including how to prioritize before you apply a single thing.

Why This Works

A job search fails in predictable ways:

  • Applications sent with no follow-up
  • Interview prep done in the car on the way there
  • Networking contacts forgotten after one exchange
  • Offers evaluated on gut feel without a framework

This template builds a system that prevents all four. Every application has a next action and a date. Every interview has a prep record. Every contact has a follow-up reminder. Every offer has a matrix.

The result is a search that feels like progress rather than chaos — and a candidate who shows up prepared at every stage.
