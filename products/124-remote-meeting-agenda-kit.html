Sales Description

Remote meetings have a reputation problem, and it's well-earned. Too many virtual calls start without a clear purpose, meander through tangents, and end with everyone wondering what was actually decided. Multiply that by five meetings a day across a distributed team, and you're burning hours of collective productivity every single week.

The Remote Team Meeting Agenda Kit fixes this by giving you battle-tested templates for every type of meeting your team runs. Each agenda is structured to keep conversations focused, ensure the right topics get covered, and produce clear action items with owners and deadlines. No more "that could have been an email" meetings.

From daily standups to weekly 1:1s, from sprint retrospectives to critical decision-making sessions, every template is designed specifically for the remote context. That means built-in accommodations for async participation, time zone awareness, and the unique communication challenges that come with not being in the same room.

Whether you're a team lead running your first remote team or a seasoned manager looking to tighten up your meeting culture, this kit gives you the frameworks to make every meeting count — and give your team back the focus time they need to do their best work.

What's Inside

  • 1:1 Meeting Templates — Structured agendas for manager-report conversations covering weekly check-ins, performance reviews, career development discussions, and difficult conversations. Includes talking point prompts, feedback frameworks, and space for both parties to add topics before the meeting.
  • Standup Agenda Formats — Ready-to-use templates for daily and weekly standups optimized for both live video calls and async written updates. Covers what was done, what's planned, and what's blocked — without letting the meeting balloon past its timebox.
  • Retrospective Frameworks — Three proven retrospective formats — Start/Stop/Continue, the 4Ls (Liked, Learned, Lacked, Longed For), and the Sailboat model — each with facilitation guides, timebox suggestions, and follow-up action templates.
  • Decision Log Templates — Document every significant team decision with context, options considered, the final choice, reasoning, and the accountable owner. Searchable and timestamped so you never have the "why did we decide that?" conversation again.
