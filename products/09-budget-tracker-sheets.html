Finally, a Budget That Actually Makes Sense

Most budgeting apps show you pretty charts after the month is over. This template helps you stay in control while it's happening.

The Personal Budget Tracker is a fully structured Google Sheets template that covers every corner of your financial life: income tracking, fixed and variable expenses, savings goals, annual trends, and a built-in debt payoff planner. You open it, plug in your numbers, and your finances become instantly readable — no formulas to write, no categories to invent, no starting from a blank spreadsheet for the third time.

What's Inside

4 interconnected sheets, pre-built and ready to use:

Monthly Budget — The core of the system. Separate sections for income (with frequency conversion so bi-weekly pay auto-calculates to monthly), fixed expenses, variable expenses with budget-vs-actual tracking, savings goals with progress percentages and months-to-target calculations, and a summary that tells you your savings rate and whether you're on track — all updated live as you enter data.

Transaction Log — A running record of every dollar in and out. Includes a category dropdown, payment method tracking, and a rolling balance column. Monthly totals calculate automatically so you can see your real spending at a glance.

Annual Overview — Twelve-month view of income, expenses, and savings side by side. Tracks category spending across the year (where did all that money go in November?), calculates year-to-date totals, monthly averages, and projected annual figures. Includes a chart data section to build an income-vs-expenses trend line chart in one click.

Debt Payoff Tracker — Enter your debts once. The tracker calculates estimated payoff dates, total interest remaining, and monthly payment totals. Includes a full explanation of the Snowball and Avalanche methods with step-by-step instructions for your specific debt list, plus a monthly payment log to track progress.

Also included: A plain-English setup guide with step-by-step import instructions, formula activation tips, how to add dropdowns, how to set conditional formatting, and a recommended monthly workflow.
