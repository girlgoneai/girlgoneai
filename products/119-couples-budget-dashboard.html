Sales Description

Money is the #1 source of conflict in relationships. Not because couples disagree about whether to save -- most agree on that. The conflict comes from the gap between shared intentions and individual visibility. One partner thinks they're spending reasonably. The other sees the credit card statement and disagrees. Nobody's tracking joint expenses consistently. Savings goals exist as vague wishes, not concrete plans. And the "monthly budget talk" keeps getting postponed because neither of you wants to start it.

The Monthly Budget Dashboard for Couples is a spreadsheet designed specifically for two people sharing finances. It tracks joint expenses with clear categorization and who-paid logging, gives each partner an individual spending allowance that doesn't require permission or justification, visualizes shared savings goals with progress bars and target dates, includes a debt payoff tracker with snowball and avalanche methods, and provides a monthly review template with guided discussion prompts so your money conversations are productive instead of accusatory.

This isn't a "one person controls the budget" tool. It's a transparent, fair system where both partners see the same numbers, contribute to the same goals, and maintain their own financial autonomy. That transparency is what turns money from a source of tension into a source of teamwork.

What's Inside

  • Joint Expense Tracker -- Every shared expense logged with date, amount, category (housing, groceries, dining, utilities, transportation, subscriptions), who paid, and split percentage. Monthly totals by category show exactly where your money goes. Filter by who paid to ensure fairness or calculate reimbursements if you split unevenly.
  • Individual Spending Allowances -- Each partner gets a personal budget -- a set amount per month that's theirs to spend however they want, no questions asked. Track personal spending separately from joint expenses. This eliminates the "why did you buy that?" conversations while keeping the shared budget on track.
  • Savings Goals Dashboard -- Set up to 5 savings goals with target amounts, target dates, and monthly contribution plans. Visual progress bars show how close you are to each goal (emergency fund, vacation, house down payment, car). Track contributions by partner so you both see who's putting in what.
  • Debt Payoff Tracker -- Log all debts (student loans, credit cards, car payments, medical) with balances, interest rates, and minimum payments. The tracker models both snowball (smallest balance first) and avalanche (highest interest first) payoff strategies with projected payoff dates. See exactly when you'll be debt-free under each approach.
  • Monthly Financial Review -- A template for your monthly money conversation with guided discussion prompts: What went well? Where did we overspend? Are our savings goals on track? Any upcoming large expenses? What adjustments do we need for next month? These prompts turn a dreaded conversation into a structured, 30-minute check-in.
