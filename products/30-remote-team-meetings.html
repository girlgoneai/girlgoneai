Sales Description

Remote meetings have a problem that no video platform can fix. People show up, talk for 30-60 minutes, and leave without a shared understanding of what was decided, who owns what, or when it's due. Notes live in someone's private doc. Action items get buried in Slack threads. Decisions made in a Tuesday standup get relitigated in Thursday's all-hands because no one can find the record.

The Remote Team Meeting System gives your team one Notion workspace for every type of meeting you run: standups, 1:1s, retrospectives, all-hands, and any custom format you need. Each meeting type has a purpose-built agenda template so you stop improvising. Every meeting generates a record: what was discussed, what was decided, and what needs to happen next. Action items are tracked in a dedicated database with owners, deadlines, and status — so nothing falls through the cracks between meetings. And a decision log gives your team a searchable archive of every choice your organization has made, with context and rationale preserved.

This template was built for remote and hybrid teams who are tired of meetings that waste time, produce no artifacts, and leave everyone wondering "wait, what did we agree on?" It works for teams of 3 and teams of 30. It works for engineering standups and leadership all-hands. It works because it imposes just enough structure to make meetings productive without making them bureaucratic.

What's Inside

3 fully-configured databases, pre-linked so meetings, action items, and decisions form a connected record of your team's operations:

  • Meetings Database — Every meeting your team runs, with pre-built agenda templates for standups (15 min), 1:1s (30 min), retrospectives (60 min), and all-hands (45 min). The Problem This Solves

Most remote teams don't have a meeting problem — they have an accountability problem. Meetings happen, but the outputs disappear. Someone took notes, but they're in a Google Doc that no one bookmarked. Action items were assigned, but they weren't tracked anywhere with a deadline. A decision was made, but three weeks later nobody remembers the rationale and the team re-debates it.

This template creates a system of record for your team's meetings. Every meeting has an agenda. Every meeting produces a record. Every action item has an owner and a deadline. Every decision has context and rationale. The result is a team that spends less time in meetings, gets more done between them, and never loses institutional knowledge.
