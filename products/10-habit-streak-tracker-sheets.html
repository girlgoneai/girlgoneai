What's Inside

5 sheets, structured for real habit building:

Habit Dashboard — Your home base. Lists up to 20 habits with category, frequency, current streak, best streak ever, completion rate percentage, and a status indicator (Active / On Fire! / Streak Master! / Struggling / Restart Needed). Summary stats at the bottom show total habits tracked, average completion rate, and your longest active streak — a quick daily gut check for where you stand.

Daily Tracker — The core of the system. A grid with habits as rows and days of the month (1–31) as columns. Mark an X when you complete a habit. Leave it blank when you miss. Daily completion counts and percentages calculate automatically at the bottom. Each habit's monthly completion rate appears at the end of its row — no manual math required. Pre-filled with 20 example habits you can replace with your own.

Weekly Review — A structured log for end-of-week reflection. Record total completions, name your top habit of the week, flag the most missed habit, and write a brief reflection note. A 4-week rolling average tracks your trajectory so you can see whether you're improving, holding steady, or slipping — before a slump becomes a pattern. Includes a weekly scoreboard breaking down performance per habit across the four weeks.

Monthly Summary — The long game. Log each month's total completions, completion rate, new habits added, habits dropped, and streak milestones hit. A Year-at-a-Glance grid shows your monthly completion rate across all 12 months of the year at a single glance. Includes a milestone log where you can record meaningful streaks as they happen.

Habit Library — 50 pre-researched habits organized into 8 categories: Morning Routine, Exercise, Nutrition, Mental Health, Productivity, Learning, Social, and Financial. Each habit includes a suggested frequency (Daily, Weekly, or 3x per week), a difficulty rating, and a brief explanation of why it works — grounded in behavioral science. Use this as your habit menu when you're ready to add something new.

The Streak Psychology (Why This Works)

The visual streak — a row of X marks with no gaps — activates a specific psychological mechanism researchers call "loss aversion." Once you've built even a small streak, the prospect of breaking it is more motivating than the goal itself. This is why the daily grid format works better than a written log or a generic to-do list.

The weekly review adds a second layer: reflection. People who review their habits weekly are significantly more likely to stay consistent than those who track without reviewing. The built-in reflection prompts take under five minutes and dramatically improve follow-through.

The 50-habit library solves the "blank page problem." Most people stall out because they don't know which habits are worth building. Every habit in the library has been selected based on its evidence base and realistic accessibility — no extreme regimens or habits that require perfect conditions.
