Sales Description

Therapy works best when you do the work between sessions. But most people walk out of their appointment with good intentions, forget what they discussed by Wednesday, and show up the following week trying to remember what they were working on. Meanwhile, the daily emotional patterns that actually matter -- the mood dips, the sleep disruptions, the triggers -- go untracked and unnoticed until they become a crisis.

The Mental Health Check-In Journal is a structured digital planner designed to sit alongside your mental health journey, whether you're in therapy, considering it, or working on your well-being independently. It includes daily mood tracking with a 5-point scale plus energy and sleep logging, CBT thought records that walk you through identifying and reframing cognitive distortions, gratitude prompts for daily and weekly reflection, a therapist session notes section with homework tracking so you never forget what you discussed, and a personal crisis plan with coping strategies, emergency contacts, and grounding techniques.

This isn't a generic journal with "write about your feelings" prompts. It's a clinically-informed tool that mirrors what therapists actually recommend between sessions -- tracking patterns, challenging thoughts, and building evidence-based coping skills.

  • CBT Thought Records -- The gold standard of cognitive behavioral therapy, simplified.   • Gratitude Prompts -- Daily gratitude entries with rotating prompts that go deeper than "what are you thankful for." Weekly reflection prompts help you identify patterns in what brings you joy, calm, and connection.
  • Therapist Session Notes -- A structured template for each session: date, topics discussed, key insights, homework assignments, and progress notes. Includes a section for questions to bring up next session so you stop forgetting what you wanted to talk about.
