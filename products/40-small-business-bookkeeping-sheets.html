Sales Description

You know how much money is in your bank account. You do not know how much of it is yours.

That's the difference between checking your balance and having a bookkeeping system. One tells you what's in the account right now. The other tells you what you've earned, what you've spent, what you owe in taxes, whether you're actually profitable, and whether you'll have enough cash to cover next month's expenses.

The Small Business Bookkeeping System is a complete financial management spreadsheet built for small business owners, freelancers, and solopreneurs who need real bookkeeping without the complexity of QuickBooks or the cost of a monthly accounting subscription. It covers everything: income and expense tracking with tax category auto-classification, an invoice log with payment status tracking, quarterly estimated tax calculations, a profit and loss statement that builds itself as you enter data, a balance sheet for tracking assets and liabilities, cash flow projections so you can see dry months before they arrive, a receipt organizer, and a mileage tracker for business travel deductions.

Every sheet is pre-built with formulas. You enter data. The reports generate themselves. No accounting knowledge required. No software to learn. Just a spreadsheet that tells you exactly where your business stands financially — today, this month, this quarter, and this year.

What's Inside

9 interconnected sheets, pre-built and ready to use:

Income Tracker — Every dollar that comes into the business. Client name, invoice reference, date received, amount, payment method, and income category (service revenue, product sales, affiliate, retainer, other). Monthly and annual totals calculate automatically. Filter by client, category, or date range.

Expense Tracker — Every dollar that goes out. Date, vendor, description, amount, payment method, and tax category. Categories are pre-configured for Schedule C (sole proprietors) and common business expense types: advertising, office supplies, software subscriptions, professional services, travel, meals, insurance, rent, utilities, equipment, education, and more. Monthly totals and category breakdowns calculate automatically.

Invoice Log — Track every invoice you send. Invoice number, client, description, amount, date sent, due date, date paid, and status (Draft, Sent, Overdue, Paid, Cancelled). Aging summary shows how much is outstanding at 0-30 days, 31-60 days, and 61-90 days. Auto-flags overdue invoices.

Tax Category Auto-Classification — Expenses are tagged with IRS Schedule C categories. At tax time, the system generates a summary by tax category so you (or your accountant) can fill in your return in minutes instead of hours. Includes a category reference guide explaining which expenses go where.

Quarterly Tax Estimates — Calculates your estimated quarterly tax payments based on year-to-date income and expenses. Uses your tax bracket and self-employment tax rate to estimate what you owe. Shows payment due dates and amounts for each quarter.

Profit & Loss Statement — Auto-generated monthly and annual P&L. Revenue, cost of goods sold (if applicable), gross profit, operating expenses by category, net profit, and profit margin percentage. All populated from your Income and Expense trackers — no double entry.

Balance Sheet — Track assets (cash, accounts receivable, equipment, inventory), liabilities (accounts payable, loans, credit cards), and equity. A simplified balance sheet format designed for small businesses, not corporate accounting departments.

Cash Flow Projections — Forward-looking cash flow for the next 6 months. Enter expected income and known expenses to see projected cash position month by month. Flags months where projected cash drops below your safety threshold. The early warning system for cash crunches.

Receipt Organizer — Log receipts with date, vendor, amount, category, and storage location (Google Drive link, photo reference, physical folder). Connects to expense entries so every expense has a receipt trail.

Mileage Tracker — Date, destination, purpose, miles driven, and IRS standard rate calculation. Running total for the year and estimated deduction value.
