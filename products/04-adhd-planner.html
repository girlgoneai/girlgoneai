What's Inside

Daily Page Template A daily planning page that starts with a brain dump zone (get it all out of your head), caps your priorities at 3 (not 10 — three), assigns energy labels to time blocks so you match tasks to your actual brain state, and ends with a done celebration section that gives your dopamine system the reward it needs.

Focus Session Tracker A pomodoro-style session log with built-in break reminders, session ratings, and notes. Tracks what you actually worked on and how it felt — so you can start noticing your own patterns over time.

Medication & Supplement Tracker Log your medications and supplements with dose and time, then rate your mood an hour later. Helps you (and your doctor) spot patterns in what's working and when.

Hyperfocus Log When you fall down a rabbit hole, log it here. Was it useful? A total detour? What do you need to redirect to? This section transforms hyperfocus from a source of shame into a source of data.

"Stuck?" Decision Tree Can't start? Can't continue? This interactive decision tree walks you through 5 types of stuck — and gives you a concrete action for each one. No vague advice. Just "here's what to try right now."

Sensory & Environment Checklist A fast pre-session checklist covering noise, lighting, physical comfort, and workspace setup. Because a dysregulated environment creates a dysregulated brain, and a quick environment scan takes 60 seconds.

Executive Function Support Task initiation prompts to use before you start anything. Transition cues for switching between tasks without losing 45 minutes to inertia. Simple, practical, immediately usable.

Weekly Review A 10–15 minute end-of-week review covering wins, struggles, patterns, medication notes, and one adjustment for next week. Designed to surface insights, not assign guilt.

Habit Tracker — ADHD Edition Start with 2–3 habits. No streaks required. Miss a day, come back tomorrow. The tracker shows you "days completed" not "days failed," and includes a non-judgmental weekly reflection prompt.

Goal Database Link your daily tasks to the deeper goals they serve. Each goal includes a "Why It Matters" field — the emotional reason, not just the logical one — because ADHD brains move toward meaning, not just obligation.
