Sales Description

Here's what happens to most people who try to get their finances organized. The Personal Finance Dashboard is a comprehensive Notion workspace that covers every dimension of your financial life — not just budgeting, but the full picture. Your net worth, tracked over time so you can see the trajectory. Your investment portfolio with performance tracking. Your debt payoff plan with actual payoff date projections. Your savings goals with progress bars that move as you contribute. Your subscriptions audited and tracked so you stop paying for things you forgot you signed up for. And a financial calendar so you never miss a bill, a quarterly tax payment, or an insurance renewal.

This is not a budgeting template. Budgeting is one piece of financial health — this system is the whole thing. If you already use our budget tracker spreadsheet (product 09), this is the next level: a connected, Notion-native system that puts your entire financial life in one view.

What's Inside

5 fully-configured databases, pre-linked so your accounts, transactions, goals, investments, and subscriptions all connect:

  • Accounts Database — Every financial account you have: checking, savings, credit cards, investment accounts, retirement accounts, loans, and property. Track balances, institution details, and calculate your net worth automatically by summing assets and subtracting liabilities.
  • Transactions Database — Your spending and income log. Categorize every transaction, track payment methods, and see monthly summaries by category. Budget-vs-actual tracking shows where you're on target and where you're overspending.
  • Goals Database — Every financial goal you're working toward: emergency fund, down payment, vacation, car, retirement milestone, debt freedom date. Track target amount, current progress, monthly contribution, and projected completion date.
  • Investments Database — Your investment portfolio broken down by holding. Track purchase price, current value, gain/loss, allocation percentage, and asset class. See your total portfolio value and performance at a glance.
  • Subscriptions Database — Every recurring charge hitting your accounts. Monthly cost, annual cost, renewal dates, cancellation deadlines, and a "keep or cancel" review status. Stop bleeding money on services you forgot about.

Dashboard with a net worth summary, monthly spending breakdown, goal progress tracker, investment performance, upcoming bills, and subscription audit — all on one page.

Debt payoff planner with Snowball and Avalanche method comparisons and projected payoff dates.

Most people know some of their numbers. They know roughly what they make. They know roughly what they spend. But they can't answer the question that actually matters: "Am I making progress?"

This template makes progress visible. Every database connects to the dashboard, and the dashboard tells you the story of your money — not just this month, but over time.

    FAQ

    Do I need a paid Notion account? The template works on Notion's free plan. A paid plan unlocks unlimited blocks and additional sharing features, but is not required.

    Is this a downloadable file? No. After purchase, you receive a Notion template link. Click the link, hit "Duplicate," and the entire system is copied to your Notion workspace in seconds.

    How is this different from the budget tracker spreadsheet (product 09)? The budget tracker spreadsheet is focused specifically on monthly income-vs-expense tracking with a transaction log and debt payoff planner. This Personal Finance Dashboard is significantly broader: it adds net worth tracking, investment portfolio management, savings goals with projections, subscription auditing, and a financial calendar. Think of the budget tracker as one chapter of your financial story; this dashboard is the whole book.

    Does it connect to my bank accounts? No. This is a Notion template with manual data entry. You update balances and log transactions yourself. While this requires more effort than auto-syncing apps, it comes with significant advantages: no third-party access to your financial data, no subscription fees, and the act of manually entering transactions increases your financial awareness (research consistently shows that people who manually track spending reduce unnecessary spending by 15-30%).

    Can I use this with my partner or spouse? Yes. Share the Notion page with your partner and you both have access to the same financial dashboard. Notion supports real-time collaboration so you can both update balances, log transactions, and track goals simultaneously.

    How often should I update it? Log transactions daily or a few times per week (5 minutes). Update account balances and net worth monthly (10 minutes). Review investment performance quarterly (15 minutes). Audit subscriptions every 3 months (15 minutes).

    What if I get stuck? The quick-start guide covers setup end-to-end. Each database includes example entries. The template is designed to be self-explanatory.
