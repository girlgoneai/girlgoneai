Sales Description

You've downloaded the habit tracker apps. You've read Atomic Habits. You've started strong on January 1st and fizzled out by February. The problem isn't motivation — it's that you're trying to brute-force behavior change without understanding the mechanics of how habits actually form. Cue, routine, reward. That's the loop. And until you design it intentionally, you're leaving your most important goals to chance.

The Habit Loop Designer is a complete behavioral psychology toolkit for building new habits and breaking old ones — using the same evidence-based frameworks that researchers and behavioral scientists use. You get habit loop mapping worksheets (cue, routine, reward), 30/60/90-day tracking grids, a habit stacking planner, implementation intention worksheets, identity-based habit design prompts, a habit scorecard, temptation bundling planner, environment design checklist, weekly review templates, and a streak visualizer.

This isn't a pretty printable with checkboxes. It's a system that helps you understand WHY you do what you do, design the specific triggers and rewards that make new behaviors automatic, and track your progress in a way that actually keeps you going. Whether you're building a meditation practice, quitting social media scrolling, or training for a marathon, this is how you make it stick.

What's Inside

  • Habit Loop Mapping Worksheets — For each habit you want to build (or break), map out the complete cue-routine-reward loop. Identify existing loops, design new ones, and find substitution patterns for habits you want to replace. Includes 10 blank loop maps plus 5 completed examples.
  • 30/60/90-Day Tracking Grids — Visual tracking grids for short-term, medium-term, and long-term habit building. Each grid includes daily check-off squares, a notes column for context, and a mini reflection prompt at each milestone (day 7, 14, 21, 30, 60, 90).
  • Habit Stacking Planner — A structured planner for chaining new habits onto existing routines using the "After I [current habit], I will [new habit]" formula. Includes a morning routine stack builder, evening routine stack builder, and a custom stack template.
  • Implementation Intention Worksheets — Fill-in-the-blank worksheets for creating specific if-then plans: "When [situation], I will [behavior]." Research shows implementation intentions double your likelihood of following through. Includes sections for obstacles, contingency plans, and commitment contracts.
  • Identity-Based Habit Design Prompts — 25 guided prompts that help you shift from "I want to run" to "I am a runner." Based on the research showing that identity-level change is the most durable form of behavior change. Includes identity statement builder, evidence log, and belief reinforcement exercises.
  • Habit Scorecard — A complete audit of your current daily habits, categorized as positive (+), negative (-), or neutral (=). Helps you see the full picture of your behavior before making changes. Includes scoring rubric and priority matrix.
  • Temptation Bundling Planner — A planner for pairing habits you need to do with activities you want to do. Based on Katy Milkman's research on temptation bundling. Includes a "want vs. need" brainstorm sheet, pairing matrix, and 30 example bundles.
  • Environment Design Checklist — A room-by-room, device-by-device checklist for redesigning your physical and digital environment to make good habits obvious and easy, and bad habits invisible and hard. Covers bedroom, kitchen, workspace, phone, and computer.
  • Weekly Review Template — A structured 15-minute weekly reflection covering what worked, what didn't, streak status, energy levels, obstacle patterns, and adjustments for the coming week. Designed to catch problems early and celebrate small wins.
  • Streak Visualizer — A large-format visual tracker (inspired by Jerry Seinfeld's "don't break the chain" method) for your most important habit. 365-day grid with monthly summaries and a year-at-a-glance view.

    Q: How many habits should I work on at once? A: We recommend starting with 1-2 habits. The system supports as many as you want, but research consistently shows that focusing on fewer habits leads to higher success rates. The habit scorecard will help you prioritize.

    Q: Can I use this digitally or do I need to print it? A: Both. The Markdown format works in Notion, Obsidian, or any text editor for digital use. If you prefer pen and paper, everything is designed to print cleanly.

    Q: What if I want to break a habit, not build one? A: The system covers both. The environment design checklist is especially powerful for breaking habits by making them invisible and adding friction.
