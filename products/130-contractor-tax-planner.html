Sales Description

If you're self-employed, taxes are a completely different beast. Nobody withholds taxes from your paychecks. Nobody sends you a neat W-2 at the end of the year. Instead, you're responsible for tracking every dollar of income, estimating and paying taxes four times a year, keeping receipts for deductions, and somehow figuring out what you actually owe — all while running your business and doing client work.

The Contractor & Freelancer Tax Planner takes this entire process and makes it manageable. Built in a spreadsheet you can start using today, it gives you a complete system for tracking 1099 income from multiple clients, calculating quarterly estimated tax payments, logging deductible expenses by IRS category, and tracking business mileage — all in one organized workbook.

The quarterly tax calculator is the centerpiece. Enter your income and deductions, and it estimates your federal, state, and self-employment tax obligations for each quarter so you know exactly how much to set aside. No more guessing, no more surprise bills in April, and no more scrambling to make estimated payments at the last minute.

Whether you're a freelance designer, a rideshare driver, a consultant, or any other flavor of self-employed, this planner keeps your tax life organized throughout the year so that when filing season arrives, you hand your accountant a clean, complete financial picture — or file confidently on your own.

What's Inside

  • Quarterly Estimated Tax Calculator — Enter your income and deductible expenses each quarter, and the calculator estimates your federal income tax, self-employment tax (Social Security and Medicare), and state tax obligations. See exactly how much to set aside and when each quarterly payment is due.
  • Deduction Tracker — Log every deductible business expense organized by IRS Schedule C categories: advertising, car expenses, contract labor, insurance, office supplies, professional services, travel, meals, and more. Include receipt references and notes for each entry so you're audit-ready year-round.
  • Mileage Log — Track every business trip with date, starting and ending location, purpose, and miles driven. The log automatically calculates your deduction using the current IRS standard mileage rate and provides quarterly and annual summaries for your tax return.
  • 1099 Income Tracker — Record income from every client with invoice dates, amounts, payment status, and running totals. View income by client, by month, or by quarter, and reconcile against 1099 forms received at year-end to ensure nothing is missed or double-counted.
  • Write-Off Category Guide — A reference section listing the most common tax deductions for freelancers and contractors, with descriptions, eligibility notes, and documentation requirements for each. Know what you can deduct, what you can't, and what records to keep for each category.
