Sales Description

Inventory management is one of those things that seems simple until it isn't. You start by keeping track of a few products in your head, then in a basic spreadsheet, and before you know it, you're overselling items you don't have, scrambling to reorder supplies, and losing money to stockouts and overstock alike. Enterprise inventory software exists, but the price tags and complexity are designed for warehouses, not small businesses.

The Small Business Inventory Tracker gives you professional-grade inventory management in a familiar spreadsheet format. No expensive subscriptions, no steep learning curves, and no features you'll never use. Just a clean, powerful system for tracking what you have, what's selling, what needs reordering, and what it all costs.

Every product gets a proper SKU entry with categories, variants, and cost data. The reorder alert system flags items approaching your minimum stock threshold so you can place orders before customers experience delays. The supplier database keeps vendor contacts, lead times, and order history organized for fast reference when it's time to restock.

Whether you sell handmade goods on Etsy, run a small retail shop, or manage supplies for a service business, this tracker brings clarity to your inventory without the overhead of complex software.

What's Inside

  • SKU Management System — Organize every product with unique SKU codes, categories, variants (size, color, material), descriptions, barcode fields, and location tags. Filter and search across your entire catalog instantly to find any item.
  • Reorder Point Alerts — Set minimum stock thresholds for each product and get visual alerts when quantities dip below your reorder point. Conditional formatting highlights low-stock and out-of-stock items so they jump off the screen before they become a problem.
  • Cost Tracking & Profit Margins — Enter purchase costs, set retail prices, and let the tracker calculate your markup percentage and profit margins automatically. See your cost of goods sold at a glance and make pricing decisions backed by real numbers.
  • Supplier Database — Maintain a directory of every supplier and vendor with company name, contact person, email, phone, website, payment terms, lead times, and order history. Link suppliers to products so you always know where to reorder from.
  • Stock Movement Reports — Track every inventory transaction — receiving new stock, fulfilling orders, returns, adjustments, and write-offs. View summary reports showing inflows, outflows, current levels, and inventory turnover rates over any time period.
