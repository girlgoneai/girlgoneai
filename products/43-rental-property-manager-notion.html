Sales Description

Property management software exists, but most of it is designed for large-scale operators and costs $20-100+/month per unit. If you're an individual landlord with a handful of rental units, you don't need enterprise software — you need a clean, organized system that tracks the things that actually matter: who's paying rent, what needs fixing, when leases expire, and whether each property is making money.

That's exactly what this Notion template does. The property portfolio dashboard gives you a bird's-eye view of your entire operation — vacancy rates, monthly cash flow, upcoming lease expirations, and overdue maintenance requests all on one screen. The tenant database keeps every detail in one place: contact info, lease terms, payment history, communication log, and move-in/move-out documentation. The maintenance request log lets you track issues from report to resolution with cost tracking and vendor contacts.

But where this template really earns its money is at tax time. The expense and income tracker is organized by property and categorized to align with Schedule E line items. When April rolls around, you're not digging through bank statements and shoe boxes full of receipts — you open the annual tax summary and everything is already sorted. Individual landlords who've used this system report cutting their tax prep time by 60-70%.

What's Inside

  • Property Portfolio Dashboard — A master overview showing all your properties at a glance: address, unit count, current occupancy, monthly rent, monthly expenses, net income, vacancy rate, and current status. Includes total portfolio metrics so you can see your overall cash flow and vacancy rate in seconds.
  • Tenant Database — A complete record for each tenant: name, contact info, emergency contact, lease start/end dates, monthly rent, security deposit, payment method, pet information, vehicle info, and a communication log. Linked to lease tracker and rent collection for seamless cross-referencing.
  • Lease Tracker — Tracks every lease with start date, end date, renewal status, rent amount, escalation terms, special clauses, and key document links. Automatic alerts for leases expiring within 30, 60, and 90 days so you're never caught off-guard by a turnover.
  • Maintenance Request Log — A ticket-style system for tracking every maintenance issue: date reported, property, unit, issue description, priority level, assigned vendor/contractor, status (reported, in progress, completed), resolution date, and cost. Includes a rolling 12-month maintenance cost view per property.
  • Rent Collection Tracker — Monthly rent tracking for each unit: due date, amount due, amount paid, date paid, payment method, late fee applied, and notes. Automatic status indicators (paid, partial, late, unpaid) and a collections summary showing current delinquency across your portfolio.
  • Expense/Income Tracker per Property — Itemized income and expense log for each property, categorized by type: mortgage/interest, property tax, insurance, repairs, maintenance, utilities, management fees, HOA, advertising, legal/professional, and other. Monthly and annual totals with per-property P&L views.
  • Vacancy Rate Monitor — Tracks occupied vs. vacant units over time, calculates vacancy rate by property and portfolio-wide, and shows average days-to-fill for vacant units. Historical data helps you identify seasonal patterns and set rental pricing.
  • Annual Tax Summary (Schedule E) — Automatically aggregates your income and expenses by property into Schedule E categories. Pulls data directly from your expense/income tracker so there's no double-entry. Includes a checklist of common deductions landlords miss.
  • Vendor/Contractor Database — Contact list for plumbers, electricians, HVAC techs, handymen, cleaners, and other service providers. Includes rates, availability, specialties, and ratings based on past work.
  • Document Links Section — Centralized links to all your key documents stored in Google Drive, Dropbox, or wherever you keep them: leases, insurance policies, inspection reports, property photos, and closing documents.
  • Quick-Start Guide — A step-by-step setup walkthrough so you can get your properties entered and start using the system in under 30 minutes.

    FAQ

    Q: How many properties can this handle? A: The template is designed and tested for 1-20 units. Notion's database system can technically handle more, but the dashboard views and calculations are optimized for individual landlords with smaller portfolios. If you have 20+ units, you may want to look into dedicated property management software.

    Q: Do I need a paid Notion account? A: No. The template works on Notion's free plan. However, if you have more than 15-20 properties, you may approach the free plan's block limits, in which case the Plus plan ($10/month) gives you unlimited blocks.

    Q: Can this replace my property management software? A: For individual landlords with 1-20 units who self-manage, yes — this handles the core functions: tenant tracking, lease management, rent collection, maintenance logging, expense tracking, and tax prep. It does NOT include tenant portals, online rent payment processing, or automated lease generation — those require dedicated software.

    Q: How does the Schedule E tax summary work? A: As you log income and expenses in the expense/income tracker throughout the year, each entry is categorized (mortgage interest, taxes, insurance, repairs, etc.) and tagged to a specific property. The annual tax summary tab automatically aggregates everything by property and category, matching Schedule E line items. You (or your accountant) can pull the numbers directly.

    Q: Can I track both residential and commercial properties? A: The template is built with residential rentals in mind, but the structure works for small commercial properties too. You may want to add custom fields for commercial-specific metrics (e.g., NNN expenses, CAM charges), which is easy to do in Notion.

    Q: Is my financial data secure in Notion? A: Notion uses industry-standard encryption and security practices. That said, this is a productivity tool, not a financial institution. Don't store sensitive information like Social Security numbers or bank account details in the template. Use it for operational tracking and keep sensitive documents in a more secure location.
