    FAQ

    Can I use this for just one or two platforms? Yes. The template is fully functional for any number of platforms. Simply add only the platform accounts you use, and filter or hide views for platforms that don't apply. Many creators start with two or three platforms and add more as they expand — the system handles both.

    Does it work for managing a client's social media accounts? Yes. You can duplicate the template once per client and customize the platform accounts, campaigns, and analytics for each. Notion also allows you to share specific pages with clients for visibility without giving them edit access to the whole workspace.

    I don't post on all five platforms. Will I still get value from this? Absolutely. The platform-agnostic structure works equally well for a creator posting on Instagram and LinkedIn only, or a business managing TikTok and YouTube. Platform properties are select fields — you only configure what's relevant to you.

    Do I need the paid Notion plan to use this? No. The template is fully functional on Notion's free plan. The free plan supports all database types, linked views, relations, rollups, and formulas used in this template. A paid plan adds unlimited blocks and more granular sharing options, which may be useful for larger teams.

    What if I want to track scheduled posts from a third-party tool like Buffer or Later? The Content Calendar includes a "Scheduled In" field where you can log which external tool (Buffer, Later, Hootsuite, etc.) has the post queued. The template doesn't replace your scheduler — it's the planning and strategy layer above it, where you decide what to create and why before the post ever reaches a scheduling tool.
