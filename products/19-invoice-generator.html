What's Inside

Three invoice templates, each designed for a different billing situation:

Standard Invoice — Clean, professional layout for project-based billing. Captures client info, a concise description of services, subtotal, tax, and total due. Ideal for fixed-price projects, one-time services, or simple retainer billing. Looks polished enough to send to enterprise clients.

Itemized Invoice — Line-item breakdown for detailed billing. List individual tasks, rates, quantities, and hours. Each row calculates its own line total. Subtotal, tax, and grand total calculate automatically at the bottom. The right choice when clients expect to see exactly what they're paying for.

Retainer / Recurring Invoice — Built for clients you bill on a repeating schedule. Tracks the current billing period, retainer amount, any rollover hours used, overage charges, and the net amount due. Includes a running log of retainer invoices sent and payments received for each client.

Payment Tracking Dashboard — All your invoices in one place. Status automatically updates to Paid, Unpaid, or Overdue based on the due date and payment date. See your outstanding receivables, total collected this month, and aged receivables (30/60/90+ days) at a glance.

Client Database Sheet — Enter each client once. Your invoice templates pull client name, address, email, and payment terms automatically using a client code — no retyping the same address on every invoice.

Year-End Summary — Monthly revenue totals, annual income total, tax collected total, and a breakdown by client. One sheet that gives you everything you need to hand to an accountant or fill out a tax return.
